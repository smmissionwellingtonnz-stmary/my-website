What we are building

More than a building. A place to belong.

The Seeds of Hope campaign is about more than purchasing a building. It is about creating a permanent home where faith can flourish, communities can gather, children can learn, and service to others can continue to grow.

We dream of a place where our children and grandchildren can grow in faith, celebrate our rich Syro-Malabar traditions, and remain connected to Christ and one another. We also envision a centre from which we can continue serving the wider Wellington community through acts of charity, compassion, and outreach.

Together, we are putting down roots

2006

The Humble Beginning

St. Mary’s Syro-Malabar Mission, Wellington began its journey in 2006 with just 15 families and 7 children attending catechism under the leadership of Fr. Joseph Akkara CMI...

At that time, the community gathered for a single monthly Holy Qurbana, celebrated at St. Francis de Sales Catholic Church, Island Bay. What began as a small gathering of faith has since grown into a vibrant and united mission community.

2009

Strengthening The Foundation

In 2009, under the leadership of Fr. Varghese Thuruthichira, a new chapter began for the community. The parish grew to 40 families with 20 catechism students...

This critical transitional phase significantly strengthened its foundation for future development. During this period, the community moved its worship to St Andrew's Church, Newlands, where it continues to gather, with Holy Qurbana celebrated twice a month.

2011

Structuring the Mission Wards

In 2011, under the leadership of Fr. Sebastian Vandanamthadathi, the parish continued to grow steadily. As the faithful expanded across the Wellington region, the community was organised into family wards...

Beginning with St. Chavara, St. Alphonsa, and St. Thomas Wards, the parish rapidly scaled to 100 families with 50 catechism students. Worship operations continued systematically at St Andrew's Church, Newlands. This period also marked the beginning of regular family ward gatherings, centred on prayer and the Rosary, strengthening fellowship within the community.

2017 - 2022

Growth, Consolidation & Testing Times

In 2017, under the leadership of Fr. Biju Xavier, the parish entered a strong phase of growth. The community expanded to over 250 families, leading to seven family wards...

The catechism programme grew to around 180–200 students, alongside active ministries such as altar service, choir, and the SMYM youth group. A formal parish council was established with regular reporting, financial accountability, and IRD charity registration.

Following the closure of St Andrew's Church, Newlands, the parish relocated to St Thomas More Church in 2018. In 2019, the community joyfully returned to St Andrew’s Church. However, when it closed again in 2022, the parish faced a challenging period without a permanent place of worship. Despite extensive efforts and appeals, no suitable space was available, and parish materials were temporarily stored in members’ homes.

2023 - 2026

The Courageous Path Forward

In 2023, the community was once again blessed to return to St Andrew's Church. In 2024, the parish took a significant step forward with the appointment of Fr. Joseph Kunnackkattu...

Under his leadership, the community entered a period of deep structural renewal, introducing ministries such as Mathruvedi, Pithruvedi, Young Couples Ministry, and multiple choir groups. Registered records climbed past 330 families and 250+ active catechism students.

In 2025, the community faced an existential crisis when the Johnsonville Parish announced the sale of St Andrew’s Church. This deeply affected the faithful, as the parish regularly gathers over 800 participants for major celebrations. In response, the parish made a courageous decision to work towards purchasing the church to secure a permanent spiritual home. In May 2026, the fundraising initiative officially launched to preserve this place of worship for generations to come.
