The Vision
Building Our Spiritual Home

A Sacred Sanctuary for Generations

St. Mary's Syro-Malabar Mission in Wellington is embarking on a faith-filled journey to establish our own church home. For years, our vibrant community has grown in grace, gathering in rented spaces. Now, the Lord calls us to plant deep roots—to acquire land and build a beautiful, traditional sanctuary that stands as a testament to our ancient faith, a spiritual home for our families, and a beacon of hope for future generations in New Zealand.

Our Story

The Growth of a Seed

2010

Humble Beginnings

A small group of Syro-Malabar Catholic families in Wellington gathered to celebrate Holy Mass in their native tongue, planting the initial spark of community fellowship.

With the blessing of local church authorities, a handful of immigrant families recognized the profound spiritual need to retain and nourish their rich St. Thomas Christian heritage. Meeting periodically, these families laid down the foundation of prayer, deep devotion, and communal support that defines our identity today.

2014

Official Recognition

The community expanded rapidly and was formalised into a community group under the Archdiocese of Wellington, establishing regular liturgical services.

As migration brought more professionals and families to the capital city, the gatherings shifted from private drawing rooms to local parish chapels. This pivotal era established structured catechism classes for children and active devotional groups, ensuring our distinctive traditions were robustly passed on.

2021

Declaration as a Mission

With immense joy, the community was formally elevated to the status of a Mission dedicated to St. Mary under the Syro-Malabar Eparchy.

This historical milestone granted our community a defined ecclesial structure, bringing a resident priest to care fully for our spiritual welfare. Our weekly Qurbana attendance reached hundreds of faithful, reinforcing our burning desire for a dedicated space to fully manifest our liturgical lifestyle.

2026

The Call to Build

We launch the "Seeds of Hope" capital project to secure land and erect our very own parish church and community hub.

Today, our thriving tree stands tall, yet its roots demand their own soil. With over 250 registered families, our reliance on rented properties restricts vital youth programmes and continuous prayer loops. We move forward blindly in faith, trusting the global diaspora to build this lasting inheritance for Christ.
