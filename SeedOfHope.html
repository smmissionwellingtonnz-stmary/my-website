— OUR STORY —

Together, we are putting down roots

2006
🌱 The Seed is Planted
St. Mary's Syro-Malabar Mission, Wellington began its journey in 2006 with just 15 families and 7 children attending catechism...

St. Mary's Syro-Malabar Mission, Wellington began its journey in 2006 with just 15 families and 7 children attending catechism under the leadership of Fr. Joseph Akkara CMI. At that time, the community gathered for a single monthly Holy Qurbana, celebrated at St. Francis de Sales Catholic Church, Island Bay. What began as a small gathering of faith has since grown into a vibrant and united mission community.

2009
🌿 First Shoots Appear
In 2009, under the leadership of Fr. Varghese Thuruthichira, a new chapter began for the community...

In 2009, under the leadership of Fr. Varghese Thuruthichira, a new chapter began for the community. The parish grew to 40 families with 20 catechism students, strengthening its foundation for future development. During this period, the community moved its worship to St Andrew's Church, Newlands, where it continues to gather, with Holy Qurbana celebrated twice a month.

2011
🍃 Branching Out
In 2011, under the leadership of Fr. Sebastian Vandanamthadathi, the parish continued to grow steadily...

In 2011, under the leadership of Fr. Sebastian Vandanamthadathi, the parish continued to grow steadily. As the faithful expanded across the Wellington region, the community was organised into family wards, beginning with St. Chavara, St. Alphonsa, and St. Thomas Wards. The parish grew to 100 families with 50 catechism students, continuing its worship at St Andrew's Church, Newlands with Holy Qurbana celebrated twice a month.

2017-2022
🌳 Strong Roots Take Hold
In 2017, under the leadership of Fr. Biju Xavier, the parish entered a strong phase of growth and consolidation...

In 2017, under the leadership of Fr. Biju Xavier, the parish entered a strong phase of growth and consolidation. The community expanded to over 250 families, leading to the formation of seven family wards and a more structured parish life.

A formal parish council was established with regular reporting, financial accountability, and IRD charity registration. Following the closure of St Andrew's Church, Newlands, the parish relocated to St Thomas More Church in 2018. In 2019, the community joyfully returned to St Andrew's Church, Newlands after its reopening, continuing its worship there until 2022.

When St Andrew's Church closed again in 2022, the parish faced a challenging period without a permanent place of worship.

2024 - Present
🌸 Blossoming & Building Our Future
In 2023, the community was once again blessed to return to St Andrew's Church, Newlands...

In 2024, the parish took a significant step forward with the appointment of Fr. Joseph Kunnackkattu. Under his leadership, the community entered a period of renewal, introducing ministries such as Mathruvedi, Pithruvedi, Young Couples Ministry, and multiple choir groups.

In 2025, the community faced a serious challenge when the Johnsonville Parish announced the sale of St Andrew's Church. In response, the parish made a courageous decision to work towards purchasing the church to secure a permanent spiritual home for future generations.

In May 2026, the parish officially inaugurated its fundraising initiative, appealing for wider support to preserve this place of worship.
