— OUR JOURNEY —

Together, we are putting down roots

2006

The Seed is Planted

St. Mary's Syro-Malabar Mission, Wellington began its journey in 2006 with just 15 families and 7 children attending catechism under the leadership of Fr. Joseph Akkara CMI. At that time, the community gathered for a single monthly Holy Qurbana, celebrated at St. Francis de Sales Catholic Church, Island Bay. What began as a small gathering of faith has since grown into a vibrant and united mission community.
2009

Roots Take Hold

In 2009, under the leadership of Fr. Varghese Thuruthichira, a new chapter began for the community. The parish grew to 40 families with 20 catechism students, strengthening its foundation for future development. During this period, the community moved its worship to St Andrew's Church, Newlands, where it continues to gather, with Holy Qurbana celebrated twice a month.
2011

The Stem Emerges

In 2011, under the leadership of Fr. Sebastian Vandanamthadathi, the parish continued to grow steadily. As the faithful expanded across the Wellington region, the community was organised into family wards, beginning with St. Chavara, St. Alphonsa, and St. Thomas Wards. The parish grew to 100 families with 50 catechism students, continuing its worship at St Andrew's Church, Newlands with Holy Qurbana celebrated twice a month. This period also marked the beginning of regular family ward gatherings, centred on prayer and the Rosary, strengthening fellowship within the community.
2017

First Leaf Unfolds

In 2017, under the leadership of Fr. Biju Xavier, the parish entered a strong phase of growth and consolidation. The community expanded to over 250 families, leading to the formation of seven family wards and a more structured parish life. The catechism programme grew to around 180–200 students, alongside active ministries such as altar service, choir, and the SMYM youth group.

A formal parish council was established with regular reporting, financial accountability, and IRD charity registration. Catechism classes were held on all four Sundays of the month, and Holy Qurbana was celebrated twice monthly, with additional services during Holy Week, Christmas, and other special occasions. To support growing parish needs, families contributed a monthly donation—initially $20, later increased to $50 as expenses grew.

Following the closure of St Andrew's Church, Newlands, the parish relocated to St Thomas More Church in 2018. In 2019, the community joyfully returned to St Andrew's Church, Newlands after its reopening, continuing its worship there until 2022.

When St Andrew's Church closed again in 2022, the parish faced a challenging period without a permanent place of worship. Despite extensive efforts and appeals to multiple churches and the Diocese of Wellington, no suitable worship space was available. During this difficult time, the community struggled to gather regularly, and parish materials were temporarily stored in members' homes to keep them safe.
2023 - Present

Blossoming Hope

In 2023, the community was once again blessed to return to St Andrew's Church, Newlands, continuing its worship with renewed joy and stability.

In 2024, the parish took a significant step forward with the appointment of Fr. Joseph Kunnackkattu, sent by the Syro-Malabar Eparchy of Melbourne. Under his leadership, the community entered a period of renewal and re-formation, introducing ministries such as Mathruvedi, Pithruvedi, Young Couples Ministry, and multiple choir groups. Family registration was also made mandatory, strengthening parish structure, with over 330 families and 250+ catechism students now actively registered.

In 2025, the community faced a serious challenge when the Johnsonville Parish announced the sale of St Andrew's Church. This deeply affected the faithful, as the parish regularly gathers over 800 participants for major Holy Qurbana celebrations and maintains six days of weekly services. With limited alternatives available, the community prayerfully and urgently explored options, seeking support from other churches and the Diocese of Wellington.

In response, the parish made a courageous decision to work towards purchasing the church to secure a permanent spiritual home for future generations. As most families are working households, this has been a significant financial challenge, yet the community continues to contribute with faith and determination. In May 2026, the parish officially inaugurated its fundraising initiative, appealing for wider support to preserve this place of worship and strengthen its mission for generations to come.

Our Goal

Building Our Home

Establish a dedicated church home where our community can gather, worship, and grow together in faith for generations to come.

Community Outreach

Support families through food drives, prayer groups, and pastoral care, extending beyond church walls to embrace the wider Wellington community.

Youth Empowerment

Nurture the next generation through catechism, youth groups (SMYM), and leadership programs that build character and faith.

Faith Formation

Deepen spiritual roots through Bible study groups, prayer meetings, and sacramental preparation programs for all ages.
