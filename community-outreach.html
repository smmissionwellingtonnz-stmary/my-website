Our Purpose

Why We Reach Out

The Community Outreach Programme was born from a simple conviction: faith must be lived in action. We mobilise volunteers to assist individuals facing hardship — from illness and isolation to unsanitary living conditions — providing practical, compassionate support with dignity and care.

Each activity is initiated in response to genuine need, carried out with full written consent, documented for transparency, and funded through the parish community.

Activity 1 · House Cleaning

House Cleaning & Rubbish Removal, Newlands

19 October 2025 1:00 PM – 6:00 PM 6 Volunteers Full PPE used indoors

A patient in her late 60s, admitted to Wellington Regional Hospital, was living in severely unsanitary conditions. Professional cleaning was unaffordable, so our volunteers stepped in before her discharge.

  • Removed ~15 large bags of rubbish from the property
  • Deep-cleaned interior rooms including bathroom and kitchen
  • PPE kits, safety boots, masks & gloves worn by all indoor volunteers
  • Green waste disposed via Hutt City Council transfer station
  • Photographs documented before and after the clean
Outcome & Impact Formally acknowledged by the surgical ward manager at Wellington Regional Hospital. The activity sparked the creation of the ongoing Outreach Programme, with a "Spin the Wheel" fundraiser raising $420 on feast day. A video presentation was shown to the parish on 23 November.

Activity 2 · Garden & Christmas

Outdoor Tidy-Up & Christmas Decoration, Taita

07 December 2025 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM 10 Volunteers Signed consent obtained

A single mother and their children, recovering from recent surgery with limited social support, requested help with their outdoor garden and Christmas preparations. Ten volunteers organised a working bee to ensure they could celebrate Christmas with joy and dignity.

  • Full lawn mowing across the property
  • Removal of overgrown trees and general outdoor clearing
  • Garden tidy-up: cutting branches, removing weeds
  • Christmas decoration installation and setup
  • Safety gloves & appropriate equipment used throughout
Outcome & Impact The family were able to enjoy a safe, beautifully tidy outdoor space and celebrate Christmas with dignity and joy. The activity deepened the parish's sense of community care and reinforced the ongoing value of this outreach ministry.
